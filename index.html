About

What is Maru?

“Maru” means round or perfection. Our extensive menu features a wide variety of the high quality sushi and sushi rolls, as well as tataki, udon, cooked appetizers and desserts for everyone.

Conveyor Belt Sushi

In South Korea, conveyor belt sushi has become popular and is known as “revolving sushi”. Kaiten-sushi is a sushi restaurant where the plates with the sushi are placed on a rotating conveyor belt that winds through the restaurant and moves past every table and counter seat. The final bill is based on the number and color of plates of the consumed sushi.

Know Your Sushi History

The origin of sushi is not Japan. It is said that sushi was introduced into Japan in the 77th century from China. People began making sushi to preserve fish by fermentation when there were no refrigerators. Since salt and rice were needed in order to ferment fish, sushi became to be closely related to rice in Japan. Then, it developed into current sushi which combines fish and rices.
